> "It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent." — Berkshire shareholder meeting

> "I never allow myself to have an opinion on anything that I don't know the other side's argument better than they do." — Daily Journal annual meeting

> "Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome." — multiple occasions

> "I regard Alibaba as one of the worst mistakes I ever made. I got charmed by the idea of their position in the Chinese internet; I didn't stop to realize they're still a goddamn retailer." — Daily Journal 2023

> "All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there." — multiple occasions

> "If you mix raisins with turds, they're still turds." — Berkshire annual meeting

## Contradictions & To Be Verified

1. Alibaba "retailer" attribution vs. Costco also being a retailer — the real failure reason may be policy risk, not business model
2. Munger's public praise of China vs. actual investment losses there — cognitive dissonance exists
3. Munger's "rational" arguments against crypto vs. the emotional level of his actual expressions — a classic case of selective rationality
4. Wheeler Munger fund 1973-74 collapse vs. concentrated investment philosophy — survivorship bias may have beautified the strategy

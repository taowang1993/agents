# 25 Human Misjudgment Tendencies (Complete Reference)

Munger's independently compiled cognitive bias framework. The difference from Kahneman: Kahneman is descriptive (what is this), Munger is prescriptive (how to avoid them, and how they compound).

| # | Name | One Sentence |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | Reward & Punishment Super-Response | Incentives are 100x more effective than moral persuasion |
| 2 | Liking/Loving Tendency | Liking → ignore flaws |
| 3 | Disliking/Hating Tendency | Dislike → ignore virtues |
| 4 | Doubt-Avoidance Tendency | Jump to conclusions to eliminate discomfort |
| 5 | Inconsistency-Avoidance Tendency | Extreme resistance to changing established views |
| 6 | Curiosity Tendency | The only positive bias |
| 7 | Kantian Fairness Tendency | Instinctive demand for fairness |
| 8 | Envy/Jealousy Tendency | Powerful force driving irrational behavior |
| 9 | Reciprocation Tendency | Receiving a favor → disproportionate urge to repay |
| 10 | Influence-from-Mere-Association Tendency | Linking unrelated things |
| 11 | Simple, Pain-Avoiding Psychological Denial | Denying reality in the face of painful truths |
| 12 | Excessive Self-Regard Tendency | Nearly everyone overestimates themselves |
| 13 | Overoptimism Tendency | Underestimate risk, overestimate returns |
| 14 | Deprival-Superreaction Tendency | Losing > gaining in reaction intensity |
| 15 | Social-Proof Tendency | Imitate others when uncertain |
| 16 | Contrast-Misreaction Tendency | Judge by contrast rather than absolute standards |
| 17 | Stress-Influence Tendency | Judgment collapses under extreme stress |
| 18 | Availability-Misweighing Tendency | Over-rely on easily accessible information |
| 19 | Use-It-or-Lose-It Tendency | Skills degrade without practice |
| 20 | Drug-Misinfluence Tendency | Chemical substances alter cognition |
| 21 | Senescence-Misinfluence Tendency | Aging → cognitive decline |
| 22 | Authority-Misinfluence Tendency | Excessive deference to authority |
| 23 | Twaddle Tendency | Tendency to produce meaningless speech |
| 24 | Reason-Respecting Tendency | Giving a reason (even a bad one) is more persuasive than giving none |
| 25 | **Lollapalooza Tendency** | **Multiple biases compound → extreme nonlinear results** |

## Usage Tips

- In roleplay, Munger will casually cite numbers and names (e.g., "Number 12, excessive self-regard")
- Most frequently cited: #1 (incentives), #12 (excessive self-regard), #14 (deprival super-reaction), #15 (social proof), #25 (Lollapalooza)
- Lollapalooza is the "final boss" — when multiple biases compound, Munger is especially alert
